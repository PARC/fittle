Plant-based Proteins

Greens: seaweed, dried spirulina, broccoli, asparagus, or artichokes (3-4 grams per serving)

Grains: amaranth, quinoa, brown rice, or sweet potato (4-5 grams per serving)

Legumes: tofu, lentils, beans, and peas (4-10 grams per serving)

Nuts and Seeds: almonds, pumpkin seeds, and flax seeds (5-9 grams per serving)

Unless you are a vegetarian or vegan, try to get most of your protein from sources like meat, poultry, and fish.

Relying on too many starches and grains as a source of protein will change the balance of your healthy plate portions. Keep in mind what a balanced, healthy plate looks like for you.

Tip: Proteins and DNA are the building blocks of all living systems, affecting every living cell in your body. Consuming protein often and consistently will help your body to build and retain lean muscle tissue, bone density, and promote healthy, lustrous hair.

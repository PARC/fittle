COMEN LA PROTEÍNA TEMPRANO Y ON FRECUENCIA

Are you meeting your exercise nutritional needs?

Team Activity

Share with the team a pic of your pre-and post-workout meal or snack. If you prefer, describe your meal and snack in some detail.

Action

If you are engaging in any purposeful exercise today or tomorrow and you don't usually fuel up before or after workouts, check out the following guidelines
  1. Roughly follow your regular eating plan. You can eat whole foods about 1 to 2 hours before exercising, and 1 to 2 hours after exercising. High-quality protein is always the priority, followed by veggies, and slow-digesting carbs.
  2. Eat what's right for your nutritional needs, especially after strength training. Bump up your protein intake for muscle retention, recovery, and improved future performance.
  3. Women should consume about 20-30 grams (palm size) of protein and men 40-60 grams (two palm sizes).
  4. If you have a hard time eating solid foods around your workouts, at the very least, consume a protein shake with a handful of greens (spinach is easy) or a green powder, and a small banana or a handful of blueberries.
  5. Start to pay attention to what works for you and what doesn't. If your stomach protests, you'll know it was not the right thing to eat.

Challenge Questions

  • How do you feel when you fuel up before your workouts? What about after your workouts?
  • If you struggle with eating around workouts, what makes it difficult? What one thing could you do today to make it just a little bit easier to get those meals in?
  • Ask the team for suggestions or ideas, if you need help. Don't struggle alone.

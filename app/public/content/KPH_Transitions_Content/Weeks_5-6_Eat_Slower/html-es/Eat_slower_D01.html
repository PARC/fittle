COMA MÁS LENTO

The second habit of the Thrive with Real Foods Challenge is to simply EAT SLOWER by purposefully and mindfully slowing down the pace at which you eat your meals. You should practice this new habit with all meals, snacks, bars, and shakes (anything that you are eating). Slow eating is one of the most powerful and positive health habits that you could ever learn. If you practice this habit consistently, it will help keep your healthier eating lifestyle on track.

Why should I eat more slowly? What's the point?

  • Can you remember the last time you rushed through a meal? How did you feel afterward?
  • If you don't remember feeling anything physically, what was your mood like? Was your mind clear and sharp or did it feel slow and sluggish, like you were in a fog?
  • Do you remember what your food smelled or tasted or looked like? What was the texture like?
  • Can you describe in some detail what your meal consisted of?
If you cannot honestly answer most of these questions in a positive light, or the entire experience escaped you, it is time to slow down. Eating is not an activity that you should do unconsciously.

What are the top three health benefits of eating slower?

  • Weight control and maintenance. By chewing your food longer, you slow down the pace at which you eat and as a result, you consume fewer calories. Many people who practice this habit consistently have gained control over their weight and manage it well over time.
  • Weight reduction and portion control. It takes the brain a full 20 minutes to "catch-up" and "register" that food has been consumed. Once the brain receives the signal, hormones are released telling you brain to turn off the hunger hormones. You've reached satiety, the feeling of fullness.
  • Better digestion and hormone regulation. The process of digestion is one of the most metabolically active and complex activities that your body performs every day, several times a day. When you slow down and chew your food thoroughly, your body can better stabilize your blood sugar and blood pressure levels, and your hormones are more balanced. You, in turn, experience fewer mood swings, heartburn, bloating, and cramping.

How do I monitor, measure, assess or gauge slow eating?

Initially, you are not required to do any measured assessment. Just report whether or not you practiced the habit. The goal is to be mindful of the act of eating, nothing more. If this is still a hard concept to grasp and you need some ways to measure this new habit try doing the following:

  • Chew your food a few more times than you normally do
  • Take smaller bites
  • Allocate an extra 5 minutes to eat each snack/meal
  • Put your utensil down between bites
  • Chew each bite of food 15-20 times
  • Take a deep breath between bites
  • Chew each bite of food until it liquefies in your mouth

Rules and strategies for Eating Slower

  • Give yourself at least 10 minutes to do nothing but eat mindfully with no distractions.
  • Sit down (no standing at the stove, sink, counter, refrigerator, etc.).
  • Take a deep breath and relax. Be grateful for the food before you. Many people in this world are food insecure.
  • Put your utensils down between bites and do not pick them up again until you have chewed your food completely and swallowed it.
  • Keep post-it notes with eat slower reminders anywhere you are likely to see them each day (i.e., refrigerator, computer, desk, etc.).
  • Set your phone or computer up to send you messaging throughout the day to remind you to eat slower.
  • Schedule time to eat meals like you would for any other appointment or activity.

What can I expect at the end of this challenge?

If you practiced the habit with some consistency and effort, you should discover that you are reaching satiety with less food and there may even be food left on your plate! More importantly, you will likely leave the table feeling a bit more energetic, light on your feet, and satisfied. Your digestive system will thank you by functioning at its optimal best and serve you well throughout the rest of your life.

To earn your activity completion for the day, try eating a snack or meal a little longer than you normally do. Employ any strategy that you can reasonably do with 90-100% certainty.

General digestive times of various foods

As a general rule for optimal health, you want your body to absorb and pass nutrients through your digestive track as fast as possible. See the quick, but an not exhaustive list of foods and their travel time in the gut below. Of course, these times will vary depending on your unique digestive health and physiology.

List of foods (liquids to solids)

  • Semi-liquid vegetables, salads, and whole raw, steamed or cooked vegetables stay in the digestive system anywhere from 20 to 50 minutes.
  • Semi-liquid fruit and whole fruit, 20 to 40 minutes.
  • Starchy grains about 60 minutes.
  • Legumes and beans about 90 minutes.
  • Seeds and nuts about 2 to 3 hours.
  • Dairy 120 minutes to 5 hours.
  • Protein shakes, eggs, fish, chicken, and turkey can pass through your system within 30 minutes to 2 hours. However, red meat (beef, lamb, and pork) can stay in the gut for 3 to 5 hours before it is broken down and leaves the body in two to three days.
Tip: Be mindful of your particular energy needs at any given time. A thumb size of nuts and seeds is a healthy snack that can carry you over until your next balanced meal.

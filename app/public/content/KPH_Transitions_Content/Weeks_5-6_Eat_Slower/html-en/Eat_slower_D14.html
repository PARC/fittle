EAT SLOWER

Eating slower is an anchor eating behavior

Congratulations! You made it through two weeks of one of the most difficult habits to master! No matter what's going on in your life or eating circumstances, you can always come back to this habit. Now that you know "how" to eat, we move on to "what" to eat.

After practicing this habit for a while, you may start to experience a wave of calmness, gratitude, and a lift in your spirits with each nourishing bite of food.

One last takeaway from this powerful habit...

Your digestive health is just as important as your mental health, so treat it with great care. Science has been telling us for some time now that our digestive system is our "second brain." Keeping a healthy digestive system is important for weight loss and general long-term health.

Tomorrow, we switch gears a bit and begin practicing a new habit. Continue on with the habit of eating slower to the best of your ability.

How do you measure a habit's success? Day 3
Share with the team habits that you feel for whatever reason may require a bit more work. Or perhaps, you felt as though you practiced the habit(s) correctly, but somehow it just didn't deliver the outcome that you had hoped.

"Subtle" yet powerful examples of successful behavior and habit changes

  • When unhealthy items find their way back into your environment, you hesitate less frequently to toss them and forget about it.
  • You now quickly look up restaurants in advance and figure out beforehand, what you will order whenever possible. If you can't always do that, your default meal is to order a salad and request a "lean and clean" serving of protein or just make the best choice that you can and eat your meal super slow and stop when you've reached satiety.
  • You eat in your car less frequently than you used to.
  • You remember to eat a small compliant meal before attending an event more often than not.
  • You drink more water, unsweetened teas, calorie-free beverages, or fewer calorie added drinks.
Please note that the habits presented in the examples are only suggestions. Feel free to discuss any habit that you'd like and share with your teammates.

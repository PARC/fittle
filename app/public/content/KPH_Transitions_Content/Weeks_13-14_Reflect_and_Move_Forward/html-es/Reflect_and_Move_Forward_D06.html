When things don't go the way we want or expect

A few days ago, we touched on some healthy indicators of progress that aren't necessarily tied to hard, measurable outcomes like reducing your weight by 30 pounds, your waistline by 6 inches or running a 5K.

While these are admirable goals, keep in mind your body is a very complicated system; always searching for homeostasis or attempting to keep things balanced internally. So, if you have been excessively depriving yourself of calories, your metabolic rate (the rate at which you burn calories at rest) will readjust to your lower caloric intake and burn fewer calories. Your weight loss slows or stops altogether, and you may even (gasp) put on weight.

The moral of the story? No matter how diligent you are with diet and exercise, the fat cells in your body will do what they were programmed to do: hold onto fat. That's why it's important to look for other ways to measure success and understand that many other positive things happening to your body as a result of consistently practicing healthier behaviors and habits.

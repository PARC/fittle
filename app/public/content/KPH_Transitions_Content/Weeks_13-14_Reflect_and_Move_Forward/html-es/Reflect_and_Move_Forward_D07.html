How do you measure a habit's success? Day 2

 Share with the team habits that you feel for whatever reason may require a bit more work. Or perhaps, you felt as though you practiced the habit(s) correctly, but somehow it just didn't deliver the outcome that you had hoped.

Example of a subtle yet powerful, successful habit using exercise

If you've been walking since the start of the weight reduction program, you may have routinely exercised for 30 minutes, four times a week, and nailed negotiating various types of landscapes (hills, sand, rocks, soft soil, etc.).

Chances are you reached those numbers by increasing your time and intensity consistently. Sure there were times when maybe you couldn't exercise at all or as hard as you would have liked, but through your resilience and perseverance, you pushed until you were back in the game. Now when confronted with new physical challenges you have the patience and fortitude to remain focused, consistent and do your best whatever the circumstances.

*Please note that the habits presented in the examples are only suggestions. Feel free to discuss any habit that you'd like and share with your teammates.

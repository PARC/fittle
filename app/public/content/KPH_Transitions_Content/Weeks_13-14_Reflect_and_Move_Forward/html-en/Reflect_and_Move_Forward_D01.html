REFLECT...THEN MOVE FORWARD

The last few weeks of the Thrive with Real Foods Challenge isn't really about learning a habit. It's more like a two-week exercise in which you will be asked to reflect and celebrate of all of your personal accomplishments and unique experiences while in the program and share with the team. By doing this, you'll create and cultivate a new vision of who you are and be able to set realistic goals for the future with a solid plan for moving forward.

Why this activity in personal reflection and team sharing is important

Research continues to show that people do much better in achieving their goals when they team up and support each other rather than going it alone.

Sharing your knowledge, expertise, and experience with your teammates who have a mutual interest and similar goals in sustaining healthy behaviors and habits, strengthens and reinforces your support network.

How the activity works

The primary goal of this practice is to get you to take pride in all that you have overcome and achieved thus far and share with others. During the next two weeks, you may learn about different ways of viewing success, progress, and achieving goals that you never considered.

We begin this practice by asking you reflect on all of the wonderful progress that you've made since the start of the program and then share it with the team. Team activities will focus on two areas:

  • Sharing with the team any of the habits that you feel are still challenging or that you did not quite master or "nail it" in the way that you had hoped. In fact, most people find that there are some habits or behaviors that still need considerable work or refining. That's okay and quite normal.
  • Sharing and participating in team activities, which describe habits that you have mastered and how you went about making that mastery happen. For example, you will be asked, "What did you do to master the habit?", "How did you do it?", and "What's your plan for building on those successes in the future?"

How to report whether or not you did the activity

Each day you are encouraged to share some of your thoughts, ideas, or feelings with the team about what this whole experience has been like for you. If there are some issues or subjects that you would prefer not share with anyone at the moment, no problem. Journal some of your thoughts in a note book for future reference and discuss them some other time if you choose.

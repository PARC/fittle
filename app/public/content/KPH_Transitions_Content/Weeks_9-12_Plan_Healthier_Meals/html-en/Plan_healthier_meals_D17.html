PLAN HEALTHIER MEALS

Eat more whole foods for optimal health

Whole foods are, at the very least, minimally refined and processed.

Lean protein, whole grains, dark or multi-colored leafy greens, root veggies, fruit, legumes, lentils, nuts, and seeds are a great start.

Tip: Need a little clarity on what exactly whole foods are? Take a moment and read what the experts have to say on the subject of whole foods in the NY Times article, " When Diet Meets Delicious."

POSITIVE DAILY ACTION

Last day of the 5, 10 or 15-minute positive daily action habit

A brief summary and a few takeaways regarding the Positive Daily Action habit:

Small positive daily action builds a strong foundation for long-term positive change. Repetition is the driving force that changes the brain and ultimately your lifestyle when you are trying to change or learn a new habit.

The brain makes no distinction between a "good" or "bad" behavior; habits are learned responses and adaptations you practice day in and day out for many years.

You can always learn new behaviors that are more in line with your deepest values and goals if you stay focused, aware, open to change, resilient, and most importantly, consistent in your desired behaviors.

Although we move onto a new habit tomorrow, strive to keep up with this daily habit of small, doable positive actions.

It's your best chance for making the sustainable and long-term changes that you dream of and work so hard for each and every day.

What is negative self-talk and how can I challenge it?

"You've been criticizing yourself for years, and it hasn't worked. Try approving of yourself and see what happens." ~Louise Hay

Why make a case for challenging negative self-talk?

Current research in neuroplasticity suggests that your thoughts, regardless if they are bullying or helpful can change the physical structure of the brain.

The problem with negative self-talk is that if you continually think hurtful or unhelpful thoughts it can, and will be harmful to you mentally and physically in the long run.

How is this possible?

Well, it turns out that what you say to yourself stimulated dopamine in the brain---the chemical responsible for habit formation and addiction. When dopamine creates a reward pathway from these types of thoughts or inner voice, you could wind up being (gulp!) addicted to negative self-talk.

Luckily, there is something that you can do about negative self-talk. Your brain is reprogrammable, and you can always learn to do something new, different, or better than you have at any time in your life.

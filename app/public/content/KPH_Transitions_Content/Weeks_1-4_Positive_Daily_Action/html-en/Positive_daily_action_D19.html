POSITIVE DAILY ACTION

Change can be slow, so consistency is really the name of the game

The only things that you can truly control are your choices and actions from moment to moment.

Many of our most entrenched habits require just as much time and effort to maintain as it does to create a new habit. For example, you could take a 5-minute smoke break or walk for 5 minutes. Same five minutes, two different activities with entirely different health outcomes.

Even if you choose to have a cigarette at some other time or on another break, you have effectively broken a link in the chain of an undesired behavior, albeit a small one, but it matters. You could easily wind up smoking one or two fewer cigarettes on any given day.

At the very least, you are now walking on purpose, and with a purpose for 10 minutes on the days you skip smoking on a break. That is certainly a change for the better and worth acknowledging.

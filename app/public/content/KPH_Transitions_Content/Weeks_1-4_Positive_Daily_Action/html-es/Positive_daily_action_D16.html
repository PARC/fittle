Your mindset determines how you view and experience change

Do you tend to view change as easy or hard and does it depend on the situation? When is change the most comfortable? When is it the hardest?

Are you sometimes too aggressive and impatient when trying to make change happen? Or are you slow to change or could it be that perhaps, you simply resist change because it feels too hard?

Today, consider how well your mindset is serving you and if perhaps, an adjustment to how you approach change is needed.

During this program, you will likely experience several forms of change, and sometimes, there will be no change at all. It's all perfectly normal and part of the process.

One way to let this whole process of change unfold a little smoother is to move your mindset towards one that is just a bit more open and fluid.

Rather than fight or resist any perceived notions that you have about how quickly change should occur, try yielding. Allow and accept that change is inevitable---and it's happening whether or not you agree with the time frame. You'll likely move towards your goals as a much saner and happier person.

Interested in learning more about exactly what is mindset?

Check out Carol Dweck's mindset online website. There's a quick test that will identify what type of mindset you have and four, simple actionable steps you can do to begin to change your mindset.

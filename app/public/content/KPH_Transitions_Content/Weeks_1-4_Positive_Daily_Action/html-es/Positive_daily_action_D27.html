ACCIÓN DIARIA POSITIVA

Reality check. "Is this strategy effective in helping me reach my desired goals and outcomes?"

"Have no fear of perfection – you’ll never reach it." ~Salvador Dali

Let's face it. Sometimes we just have to accept the fact that our old way of doing things just doesn't work any longer or perhaps, it never really worked all that well in the first place.

Today, think about some entrenched strategies, habits, behaviors, or actions that you currently employ that may appear to have outlived their usefulness. Before you make the decision to put them on the chopping block, consider that perhaps the goal or desired outcome is too big for your current skill set and needs further breaking down into smaller pieces.

A few things to consider when deciding whether to stick with a habit or behavior

  1. Does your goal need to be broken down into smaller, doable chunks? Let's say you would like to prep five or six meals for the week, but you feel overwhelmed even thinking about it, let alone finding the time to do it. Why not start with prepping and preparing snacks for a few days while you are cooking a meal? That is "one" small action that you can do in 5 to 15-minutes to support your overall strategy of prepping meals or snacks in advance. You are now a little closer to the desired outcome.
  2. Maybe it's time to ditch what doesn't currently work for your long-term goals of weight reduction. Some examples might be, consuming too few calories, eating only once a day, exercising harder and harder or trying an array of extreme diets that limits certain foods. These all work in the short-term (or not); however, they are completely unsustainable.
  3. Use an outcome-based decision-making process: you decide how to move forward based on the real-life evidence you've gathered and how it works with all of the other previous decisions you've made thus far.
The bottom line: Make sure that you are getting the kind of results or outcomes that you want from your current behaviors, actions, and habits. If not, give everything a hard look, do your own experiments to test what you are actually doing and then decide if you are headed in the right direction.

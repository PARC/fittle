Dining out strategies to stay on track (continued from yesterday)

Yesterday, we discussed some of the information you'll need to start crafting reliable dining out strategies. Today, let's discuss how you might go about executing those strategies.

Step 3 - Execute a strategy

  1. Take a moment and carefully think about your personal triggers and cues that might prompt you to overeat or make less than healthy choices (e.g., your physical and mental state, people, places, etc.)
  2. Write down your triggers and cues, followed by some realistic actions that you could take to either respond or avoid them altogether.

Step 4 - A few days before the event

  1. Practice asking for substitutions or any special requests to get over any nervousness about speaking up for what you'll need to make your meals a bit friendlier to your new style of eating.
  2. Spend roughly 5 minutes a day visualizing a scenario in which you are successfully implementing your strategies with ease, and thoroughly enjoying the experience and company of others.

Consider and commit to trying any one of these suggested strategies, or one of your own the next time a dining out opportunity presents itself.

Tip: Using imagery as a form of mental training is a proven technique for successful outcomes employed by Olympians and World-Class Athletes everywhere!

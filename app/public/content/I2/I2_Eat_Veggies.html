EAT MORE VEGETABLES

This habit is about getting yourself to to eat at least 5 servings of vegetables each day as advised by the Centers for Disease Control & Prevention (CDC) to help reduce your risk of various chronic diseases like stroke, type 2 Diabetes, some types of cancer, and even heart disease.

To get started on this habit right away, choose as many veggies as you would like from the list below and include them in your diet as indicated in the daily tip from PARC Coach.

Veggie List

  • Asparagus
  • Broccoli
  • Brussels Sprouts
  • Cabbage
  • Carrots
  • Cauliflower
  • Celery
  • Collard Greens
  • Cucumber
  • Eggplant
  • Fennel

How does this habit work?

  • The basic idea of is to get up to at least 5 servings of veggies into your body however you can each and every day as recommended by the CDC.
  • PARC Coach will help you build this habit by providing you a daily tip about how to add more vegetables to your diet each day.
  • At the end of the study, you should be eating a minimum of 5 servings of veggies per day OR at least increased your daily intake by a few servings.

How much is a vegetable serving?

  • A serving size is approximately half a cup cooked or one cup of raw veggies like spinach, kale or lettuce. The size of your fist is also about one serving.
  • If you still need help with determining appropriate portion sizes, use your healthy plate diagram.

What about fruit, can I have it and how much?

  • Yes, of course you can have fruit in its whole form, but consume it in moderation (2-3 servings per day). Fruit is loaded with nutrients, anti-oxidants, and fiber.
  • Stick with berries (strawberries, gooseberries, blueberries) and stone fruits like apples, plums, and pears.
  • Cucumbers, peppers, tomatoes and squash are also fruits. A medium sized piece of fruit like an apple or plum is one serving size.

BEFORE YOU BEGIN PRACTICING THIS HABIT PLEASE NOTE:
Leafy greens have blood clotting properties (vitamin K), so if you are on blood thinners, be safe and check in with your doctor before increasing your daily vegetable consumption.

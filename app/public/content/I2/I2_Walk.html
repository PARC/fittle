WALK MORE

This card will give tell you briefly about the health benefits of exercising by walking regularly.

How does this habit help you?

Reduce health risks

There are countless health benefits to walking on a regular basis. It is the most undervalued exercise in our society. The American Heart Association and the College of Sports Medicine recommend walking as an exercise intervention that can help with a number of ailments. For beginners, it can reduce health risks for the following:

  • Heart and lung disease
  • Type 2 diabetes by 58%
  • High cholesterol
  • Arthritis
  • Mild depression symptoms by 47%
  • High blood pressure
  • Stress
  • Obesity
  • Fat loss

Positively impact other health parameters

  • Weight maintenance
  • Improved metabolic response to exercise
  • Confidence
  • Self-image
  • Feelings of well-being
  • Bone density
  • Osteoporosis (which affects 15 million Americans)

Recommended walking guidelines

  • USDA, AHA and CDC guidelines recommend at least 30 minutes (5 days a week) as the minimum standard. Walking does not have to be vigorous to reduce cardiovascular risk factors. Amazingly, that even includes strolling.
    *USDA: US Department of Agriculture; AHA: American Heart Association; CDC: Centers for Disease Control & Prevention
  • One study looked at 17,000 alumni from Harvard over 20 years. The conclusion was that for walking for 30 minutes, 5 days per week could reduce the risk of heart attack by 28% or more.

Important health instructions

Do not start any exercise program if you are not sure of your medical condition. Please see your physician if you have any questions or concerns before starting this program.

  • Make sure you have proper attire for the weather and a water bottle to prevent dehydration.
  • Remember to always start your workout with a sufficient warm-up. And finish off your workout with a cool-down. You can do simple stretching exercises of the legs to warm up and cool down the leg muscles that are active during walking.
  • Suitable walking shoes or sneakers are mandatory.
  • If at all possible, walk on softer surfaces, such as walking trails or the beach. It is less stressful on the joints, particularly if you are older or overweight. Eventually, it will get easier if you stick with the program.
  • Keep in mind that your safety always comes first! Do only as much as you can tolerate without any pain or discomfort.

Caution

  • Do not overdo it. If you get a little faint, stop and take a break. This is not a race -- your goal is to develop a healthy exercising habit.
  • The prize is the improved feeling of wellbeing and health you will get if you complete the program and continue walking regularly after 4 weeks.

Cardio: Seated March

This exercise is to build the endurance and strength in your legs. Perform 3 sets of 45 seconds.

Equipment:

Sturdy chair

*Note- You will feel your breathing rate and heart rate increase slightly during this exercise.  You may also feel the muscles in the front of your hips working.

Instructions:

  1. Start seated tall in the chair.
  2. Scoot forward slightly, so you are not resting your back in the back rest.
  3. Keeping the knees bent, alternate raising and lowering one knee at a time.
  4. Continue the exercise for 45 seconds.
  5. Repeat 2 more times. Remember you need to perform 3 sets of 45 seconds.

Making it harder (more challenging):

  1. Start seated tall in the chair.
  2. Scoot forward slightly, so you are not resting your back in the back rest.
  3. Keeping the knees bent, alternate raising and lowering one knee at a time.
  4. Continue the exercise for 60 seconds.
  5. Repeat 3 more times. Remember you need to perform 3 sets of 45 seconds.

 

Making it easier (less challenging):

  1. Start seated tall in the chair, but you may rest your back on the backrest.
  2. Keeping the knees bent, alternate raising and lowering one knee at a time.
  3. Continue the exercise for 30 seconds.
  4. Repeat 2 more times. Remember you need to perform 3 sets of 45 seconds.

Lower Body: Quad Exercise

 

This exercise is to strengthen the muscles in your thigh. Perform 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions on each leg.

 

Equipment:

 

Sturdy chair, theraband (more challenging only)

 

*Note- You will feel the muscles in the front of your thigh working.

 

Instructions:

 

  1. Begin by sitting in the chair with your feet firmly placed on the ground.
  2. Straighten one leg out in front of you all the way, tightening the muscles in the thigh.
  3. Return the foot back to the ground.
  4. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 10-15 repetitions on each leg.

 

Making it harder (more challenging):

 

  1. Tie the theraband to the back leg of the chair.
  2. Sit in the chair and loop one foot through the theraband.
  3. Straighten one leg out in front of you all the way, tightening the muscles in the thigh.
  4. Return the foot back to the ground.
  5. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 10-15 repetitions on each leg.

 

Making it easier (less challenging):

 

  1. Begin seated in the chair with your feet placed on the ground.
  2. Tighten the muscles in the thigh to try to straighten your leg in front of you as much as you can.
  3. Return the foot back to the ground.
  4. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 10-15 repetitions on each leg.

Balance: Single Leg Balance

This exercise is to improve your balance and the muscles in your legs that help with balance. Perform 3-5 sets on each leg.

Equipment:

Sturdy chair (or can perform at counter top for more stability)

*Note- You will feel the muscles in your ankles and hip working.  

Instructions:

  1. Stand tall holding on to the chair or counter.
  2. Bend on knee, so you are standing on one leg.
  3. Try to hold for 30 seconds, then switch legs.
  4. This is one repetition. Remember you need to perform 3-5 sets on each leg.

Making it harder (more challenging):

  1. Stand tall holding on to the chair or counter.
  2. Bend on knee, so you are standing on one leg.
  3. Once you have your balance, try to lift one hand up and eventually both hands.
  4. Try to hold for 30 seconds, then switch legs.
  5. This is one repetition. Remember you need to perform 3-5 sets on each leg.

Making it easier (less challenging):

  1. Stand tall holding on to the chair or counter.
  2. Bend on knee, so you are standing on one leg.
  3. Try to hold for 10-15 seconds, then switch legs.
  4. This is one repetition. Remember you need to perform 3-5 sets on each leg.

Upper Body: Arm Exercise

This exercise is to strengthen the muscles in your arms and shoulders. Perform 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions of this exercise.

Equipment:

Sturdy chair, empty gallon jug (or other weighted object)

*Note- You will feel the muscles in the front of your shoulders and arms working during this exercise.

Instructions:

  1. Fill the gallon jug 50% full of water (or grab another similar weight object).
  2. Start seated tall in the chair with your object in your lap.
  3. With your arms straight in front of you, raise the object to chest height, and back down to your lap.
  4. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

Making it harder (more challenging):

  1. Fill the gallon jug 75%-100% full of water (or grab another similar weight object).
  2. Start seated tall in the chair with your object in your lap.
  3. With your arms straight in front of you, raise the object to chest height, and back down to your lap.
  4. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

Making it easier (less challenging):

  1. Fill the gallon jug 25% with water (or perform exercise without weight).
  2. Start seated tall in the chair with your object in your lap.
  3. With your arms straight in front of you, raise the object to chest height, and back down to your lap.
  4. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

Flexibility: Core Exercise

This exercise is to strengthen the muscles in your trunk (abdomen and back). Perform 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

 

Equipment:

 

Sturdy chair, weighted object (ie, half gallon of water, for progression only)

 

*Note- You will feel the muscles in your stomach working.

 

Instruction:

 

  1. Start seated tall in the chair.
  2. Scoot forward about 6 inches so your back is not resting on the chair.
  3. Cross your arms over your chest.
  4. Lean back to tap your shoulders on the backrest.
  5. Return to sitting tall.
  6. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

 

Making it harder (more challenging):

 

  1. Start seated tall in the chair.
  2. Scoot forward about 6 inches so your back is not resting on the chair.
  3. Hold onto a weighted object, like a half gallon of water.
  4. Lean back to tap your shoulders on the backrest.
  5. Return to sitting tall.
  6. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

 

Making it easier (less challenging):

 

  1. Start seated tall in the chair.
  2. Scoot forward about 6 inches so your back is not resting on the chair.
  3. Rest your hands onto your knees.
  4. Lean back to tap your shoulders on the backrest.
  5. Return to sitting tall, using your hands to gently assist you if needed.
  6. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

Lower Body: Sit to Stand Exercise

This exercise is to strengthen the muscles surrounding your hip and knee joints. Perform 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions of this exercise.

Equipment:

Sturdy Chair, 1-2 firm pillows or cushions (only needed for the regression)

*Note- You should feel the muscles on the front and back of your thighs working during this exercise.

Instructions:

  1. Place a sturdy chair with the back against the wall (to keep it from moving).
  2. Start sitting up tall in the chair.
  3. Lean your body weight forward and come into standing.
  4. Slowly return to sitting in the chair.
  5. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

Making it harder (more challenging):

  1. Place a sturdy chair with the back against the wall (to keep it from moving).
  2. Start sitting up tall in the chair.
  3. Lean your body weight forward and come into standing.
  4. Slowly return towards a seated position but only briefly tapping your bottom on the chair before standing again.
  5. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.

*You can also make this exercise more difficult by keeping your hands in front of you instead of using them to help you push up from the chair.

Making it easier (less challenging):

  1. Place a sturdy chair with the back against the wall (to keep it from moving).
  2. Place 1-2 firm pillows or cushions on the seat of the chair to make it taller.
  3. Start sitting up tall in the chair.
  4. Lean your body weight forward and come into standing.
  5. Slowly sit back down in the chair.
  6. This is one repetition. Remember you need to do 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions.
